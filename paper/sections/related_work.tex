\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}

\paragraph{Feed-forward sparse-view geometry.}
Classical multi-view reconstruction and SLAM rely on correspondence, triangulation, and bundle
adjustment over calibrated or densely overlapping views (e.g.\ COLMAP~\cite{schonberger2016sfm},
ORB-SLAM3~\cite{campos2021orbslam3}). Feed-forward models remove the per-scene optimization: DUSt3R
predicts point maps from unconstrained images~\cite{wang2024dust3r}, MASt3R adds 3D-aware
matching~\cite{leroy2024mast3r}, and VGGT predicts cameras, depth, point maps, and confidence from one
or many views~\cite{wang2025vggt}. We use VGGT as a \emph{frozen} source of pixel-aligned 3D evidence,
but our output is an object--relation graph, not a reconstruction.

\paragraph{Open-vocabulary detection as a labeling front-end.}
A lifted scene graph is only as good as its 2D labels. The common mask-then-embed recipe pairs
class-agnostic SAM masks~\cite{kirillov2023segment} with per-region CLIP
embeddings~\cite{radford2021clip} (optionally DINOv2~\cite{oquab2023dinov2}); it is fully frozen but
pushes naming onto whole-image-trained CLIP applied to crops, which tends to return scene-scale guesses
rather than the objects present. Open-vocabulary \emph{detectors} instead localize and name in one
pass --- OWL-ViT~\cite{minderer2022owlvit}, OWLv2~\cite{minderer2023owlv2}, Grounding
DINO~\cite{liu2024groundingdino}, Detic~\cite{zhou2022detic}. We adopt OWLv2 because it emits
localized, named instances --- the property the lifting and fusion stages need --- and study what that
buys a sparse-view 3D pipeline over mask-then-embed.

\paragraph{Open-vocabulary 3D semantics.}
Open-vocabulary 3D understanding typically lifts or distills foundation-model features into point
clouds, maps, or neural representations: building on NeRF~\cite{mildenhall2020nerf}, LERF embeds
language in radiance fields~\cite{kerr2023lerf}, while OpenScene~\cite{peng2023openscene},
ConceptFusion~\cite{jatavallabhula2023conceptfusion}, OpenMask3D~\cite{takmaz2023openmask3d}, and
OpenSplat3D~\cite{piekenbrinck2025opensplat3d} fuse open-set features over points, maps, masks, or
Gaussian splats~\cite{kerbl2023gaussian}. We are complementary: we build explicit object--relation
graphs from sparse RGB views without a completed 3D map or a per-scene radiance field. Notably,
uncertainty-aware aggregation has been proposed for multi-view fusion in this
setting~\cite{li2025cuao3d}; we revisit it on real detector labels under an independent reference and
report a negative result (Section~\ref{sec:results}).

\paragraph{3D scene graphs.}
3D scene graphs organize objects and their relations into structured scene
representations~\cite{armeni2019scenegraph}, with datasets and learned predictors for reconstructed
indoor scenes~\cite{wald2020threedssg,dai2017scannet}. ConceptGraphs is closest to our output: it builds
open-vocabulary 3D scene graphs from \emph{posed RGB-D} sequences~\cite{gu2024conceptgraphs}. We share
the object--relation goal but relax the input to sparse RGB views lifted through feed-forward geometry.
A recurring failure of any such lifting-and-fusion pipeline is \emph{over-counting} --- one object
surviving as several nodes --- which motivates our duplicate-aware merge.

\paragraph{Honest evaluation and reproducibility.}
With no clean instance-level ground truth for a few casual RGB views, it is tempting to score a method
against a reference derived from its own dense-view output --- coupling reference to predictor and
producing a \emph{circular} evaluation in which a method scores perfectly at the reference view by
construction. Such reference--predictor coupling is a form of information leakage, a known driver of the
reproducibility crisis~\cite{kapoor2023leakage,gundersen2018reproducibility}. The 3D community has
responded with controlled benchmarks carrying independent ground truth (e.g.\
Replica~\cite{straub2019replica}, TUM RGB-D~\cite{sturm2012tumrgbd}). In that spirit we contribute an
independent sparse-view reference authored from the frames, document the circular-pseudo-reference
pitfall, and report what actually helps once it is removed.
